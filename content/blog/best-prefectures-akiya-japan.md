# Best Prefectures to Buy Akiya in Japan — Where to Look in 2026

Japan has 47 prefectures, and akiya exist in every single one. But not all locations are equal. Price, accessibility, climate, community support, and lifestyle vary dramatically depending on where you look.

This guide compares the best prefectures for akiya buyers in 2026, based on real data and practical considerations.

---

## How We Ranked These Prefectures

We evaluated each prefecture on five criteria:

- **Price** — Average akiya cost and availability of free/ultra-cheap properties
- **Accessibility** — Distance to major cities, airports, and shinkansen stations
- **Climate** — Temperature range, snowfall, humidity, and natural disaster risk
- **Lifestyle** — Local culture, food, outdoor activities, and expat community
- **Support** — Municipal subsidies, akiya bank quality, and foreigner-friendliness

---

## 1. Nagano Prefecture — The All-Rounder

**Why Nagano:** Consistently the most popular prefecture for akiya buyers, both Japanese and foreign. It offers the best balance of affordability, accessibility, and lifestyle.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 500,000 - 5,000,000 yen |
| Access to Tokyo | 80 minutes by Hokuriku Shinkansen |
| Climate | Four distinct seasons; cold winters with snow in mountain areas |
| Highlights | Ski resorts, hiking, hot springs, apple orchards, craft beer scene |
| Subsidies | Many municipalities offer 500,000 - 2,000,000 yen renovation grants |

**Best areas:** Ueda, Azumino, Omachi, Karuizawa (higher prices but resort town appeal), Togakushi

**Who it is for:** People who want nature without total isolation. Great for remote workers, outdoor enthusiasts, and families.

---

## 2. Niigata Prefecture — Maximum Value

**Why Niigata:** Some of the cheapest akiya in Japan, combined with stunning natural scenery and world-class rice and sake.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 100,000 - 3,000,000 yen |
| Access to Tokyo | 2 hours by Joetsu Shinkansen |
| Climate | Heavy snowfall in winter (2-3 meters in some areas); warm, humid summers |
| Highlights | Rice paddies, sake breweries, skiing, Sado Island, onsen |
| Subsidies | Aggressive depopulation countermeasures; generous relocation packages |

**Best areas:** Myoko, Tokamachi, Tsunan, Murakami

**Who it is for:** Budget buyers who do not mind heavy snow. The extremely low prices make Niigata ideal for first-time akiya buyers or those with limited renovation budgets.

**Important note:** Snow country means you need a car, snow tires, and budget for heating. Roofs must be maintained to handle snow load.

---

## 3. Ehime Prefecture — The Mild Climate Choice

**Why Ehime:** Located on Shikoku island, Ehime offers warm weather year-round with minimal snowfall. Known for its laid-back lifestyle, citrus orchards, and excellent cycling routes.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 300,000 - 4,000,000 yen |
| Access | Matsuyama Airport (domestic flights); ferry to Hiroshima |
| Climate | Mild year-round; warm winters, minimal snowfall |
| Highlights | Shimanami Kaido cycling route, Dogo Onsen, mikan oranges, quiet beaches |
| Subsidies | Multiple municipalities with active akiya programs |

**Best areas:** Imabari, Saijo, Uchiko (traditional townscape), Uwajima

**Who it is for:** People who dislike cold weather. Excellent for retirees, cyclists, and those seeking a slow-paced coastal lifestyle.

---

## 4. Oita Prefecture — Hot Springs Paradise

**Why Oita:** Home to Beppu, the hot spring capital of Japan. Oita combines affordability with unique geothermal culture and a growing international community.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 300,000 - 5,000,000 yen |
| Access | Oita Airport; JR lines to Fukuoka (2 hours) |
| Climate | Mild and humid; warm winters except in mountain areas |
| Highlights | Onsen everywhere, Yufuin resort town, Kunisaki Peninsula temples |
| Subsidies | Active relocation support programs in rural municipalities |

**Best areas:** Bungotakada, Kitsuki (castle town), Taketa, Usuki

**Who it is for:** Onsen lovers and those who want a warm climate in Kyushu. The international community in Beppu makes it easier for foreigners to settle in.

---

## 5. Akita Prefecture — The Budget Champion

**Why Akita:** Some of the cheapest properties in Japan, including many free akiya. Akita faces severe depopulation, which means local governments are highly motivated to attract new residents.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 0 - 2,000,000 yen |
| Access | Akita Shinkansen from Tokyo (4 hours); Akita Airport |
| Climate | Heavy snowfall; cold winters; beautiful but harsh |
| Highlights | Samurai town Kakunodate, kiritanpo cuisine, Lake Tazawa, festivals |
| Subsidies | Among the most generous in Japan; some towns offer free land + building subsidies |

**Best areas:** Yokote, Daisen, Semboku (Kakunodate area)

**Who it is for:** Serious budget buyers willing to embrace rural life and cold winters. Akita rewards those who commit — the community support and subsidies are exceptional.

---

## 6. Okayama Prefecture — The Sunshine Belt

**Why Okayama:** Known as "the Land of Sunshine" for having the most clear-sky days in Japan. Okayama sits on the Sanyo coast between Osaka and Hiroshima, with excellent rail connections.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 500,000 - 5,000,000 yen |
| Access | Okayama Station (major shinkansen stop); 45 min to Osaka by Nozomi |
| Climate | Warm and dry; least rainfall of any major prefecture |
| Highlights | Kurashiki historical district, peach and grape orchards, Seto Inland Sea islands |
| Subsidies | Moderate; varies by municipality |

**Best areas:** Takahashi, Maniwa, Bizen, Setouchi (coastal)

**Who it is for:** People who want sunshine and good shinkansen access. Okayama is an excellent base for those who need to travel to Osaka, Kyoto, or Hiroshima regularly.

---

## 7. Hokkaido — Wide Open Spaces

**Why Hokkaido:** Japan's northern frontier offers vast landscapes, large properties, and a completely different atmosphere from mainland Japan. Hokkaido akiya often come with significantly more land than properties elsewhere.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Average akiya price | 500,000 - 5,000,000 yen |
| Access | New Chitose Airport (Sapporo); domestic flights from all major cities |
| Climate | Cold winters (-10 to -20C); cool, pleasant summers; heavy snow |
| Highlights | Powder snow, lavender fields, dairy farms, seafood, wide roads |
| Subsidies | Varies widely; some towns actively recruit settlers |

**Best areas:** Furano/Kamikawa area, Niseko surroundings (higher prices but tourism potential), Tokachi, Rumoi

**Who it is for:** People who want space, nature, and do not mind long winters. Hokkaido properties often include large yards or even farmland. Ideal for those with a homesteading mindset.

## How to Choose Your Prefecture

1. **Start with climate** — Can you handle heavy snow? Or do you need warmth year-round?
2. **Check accessibility** — How often will you travel to major cities or airports?
3. **Visit first** — Spend at least a few days in your target area before buying
4. **Talk to locals** — Visit the municipal office and ask about akiya programs and community support
5. **Think long-term** — Consider healthcare, shopping, and social connections, not just the property itself
